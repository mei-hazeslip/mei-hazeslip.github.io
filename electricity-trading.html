Electricity Trading

Electricity Price Spread Direction Prediction

Background

High-electricity-consumption companies can reduce costs by deciding how much electricity to purchase in the day-ahead market versus the real-time market. Each morning, companies submit hourly demand for the next day, but both the final day-ahead and real-time prices are still uncertain at the time of submission.

This project predicts whether the real-time electricity price will be lower than the day-ahead price for each hour of the next day. If real-time prices are expected to be lower, a company may reduce its day-ahead commitment and settle more usage in the real-time market; if real-time prices are expected to be higher, it may declare more day-ahead quantity to reduce price risk.

Data & Features

The dataset contained hourly electricity-market observations from April to July 2025, including day-ahead forecasts, recent real-time variables, renewable generation, load, bidding space, interconnection, and price history. I engineered time features, lag features, price-difference features, ramp features, and market-balance indicators.

Exploratory Data Analysis

The target showed clear intraday structure, especially around ramping periods, suggesting that time-aware modeling was necessary (Figure 2). Several market and forecast variables showed different distributions between positive and negative spread-direction outcomes, motivating feature engineering around load, bidding space, renewable generation, and recent prices (Figure 3).

Modeling & Validation

I implemented a machine-learning workflow with data preprocessing, feature engineering, and model training using LightGBM, XGBoost, CatBoost, and Logistic Regression. A rolling month-based validation was used to mimic the real-world forecasting scenario and evaluated performance using ROC-AUC.

Results

The best LightGBM feature set achieved approximately 0.60 mean validation ROC-AUC across rolling monthly splits, improving over the 0.50 no-skill baseline. A final rank-blended LightGBM prediction was used to produce probability-based submissions.
