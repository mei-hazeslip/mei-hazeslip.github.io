Electricity Trading

Electricity Price Spread Direction Prediction

Background

High-electricity-consumption companies can reduce costs by deciding how much electricity to purchase in the day-ahead market versus the real-time market. Each morning, companies submit hourly demand for the next day, but both the final day-ahead and real-time prices are still uncertain at the time of submission.

This project predicts whether the real-time electricity price will be lower than the day-ahead price for each hour of the next day. If real-time prices are expected to be lower, a company may reduce its day-ahead commitment and settle more usage in the real-time market; if real-time prices are expected to be higher, it may declare more day-ahead quantity to reduce price risk.
